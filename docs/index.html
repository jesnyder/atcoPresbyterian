1851PastorFirst Presbyterian Preaching Begins in Waterford

Rev. Charles Ford, pastor of the Presbyterian Church of Williamstown, begins occasional preaching in the Waterford area. The Domestic Missionary Rev. Allen H. Brown also preaches here for approximately fourteen to fifteen years. No regular services are yet established. Rev. Ford ceases preaching around 1853 and returns again in 1861.

1861–1865Historical ContextThe Civil War Era

Our story begins against the backdrop of the United States Civil War — a time of great national healing and spiritual revival. Andrew Johnson (born 24 Dec 1808, died 15 July 1875), the 17th President, serves from 1865–1869. The period of Reconstruction that follows helps shape the communities of southern New Jersey, including Atco and Waterford.

c. Oct 1866OrganizationRev. Brace Begins Waterford Services; Plans Made for a Church Building

About October 1866, Rev. F.R. Brace — who also labors at Hammonton, Elwood, Pleasant Mills, and Batsto — commences holding services once every two weeks in the Waterford Church building, shared in communion with the Methodist Society. As growth at Hammonton demands two services per Sabbath, Rev. Brace can no longer preach mornings at Waterford. A proposal to share the meetinghouse (with morning services available to Presbyterians) is declined by the Methodist Society.

Approximately $750 is contributed toward a dedicated Presbyterian building. A fair and festival raises an additional $250. Key donors: William Porter (lot, all siding, and glass), Andiciot Hay of Winslow (all shingles). Committee members appointed: William La Porter · William Robinson · Samuel S. Stiles · Everett Battelle · James Mac Dougall.

1866OrganizationThree Meetings Lead to the Organization of the Atco Church

The first meeting to consider organizing a Presbyterian church in Atco assembles in the hall above Wellington & William “Baker’s Brother Store” (southeast corner of Atco Ave & Raritan Ave., now owned by Mr. Charles A. Woodland, Esq.). A second meeting is held in the parlor of Mr. John E. Hand’s house (Atco Ave & Fifth Street). At a third meeting in the “Atco House” hotel (corner of Atco Ave & Atlantic Ave — the first hotel in Atco, built by Thomas Richards Jr., later destroyed by fire), the organization of the First Presbyterian Church of Atco is consummated under the care of the Fourth Presbytery of Philadelphia, PA (New School).

First Board of Trustees: Mr. Thomas Richards Jr. · Mr. Peter McHargh · Mr. M.A. Wakelee · Mr. W.O. Talcott · Mr. Henry A. Green

Note: Around 1827, Thomas Richards Jr. had founded the Jackson Glass Works and established the Atlantic Transportation Company “ATCO,” bringing the Camden & Atlantic Railroad through the area — giving Atco its very name.

25 Apr 1866WaterfordFirst Presbyterian Church of Waterford Formally Organized

The Presbytery committee meets at Waterford at 3 PM with Rev. Charles Ford as Moderator. Charter members: Everett Battelle (ordained Ruling Elder, 5 Nov 1869) · James MacDougall & wife Eliza · William Robinson & wife Calcina · Caroline Barnard · Miss Mary H. Porter · Alexander Heggan. William Robinson is unanimously elected Senior Ruling Elder.

Nov 1867FoundingFirst Presbyterian Church of Atco Formally Organized — 13 Charter Members

On November 17th and 24th, 1867, meetings result in the official organization of the First Presbyterian Church of Atco under the 4th Presbytery of Philadelphia, PA (New School). Mr. Clinton Dewitt Carpenter and Mr. James E. Alton are elected as Ruling Elders. Original Charter Members: Mrs. Mary R. Loomis · Mr. C. DeWitt Carpenter · Mrs. A. Carpenter · Professor Henry A. Green · Mr. J.E. Alton · Mrs. L. Alton · Mrs. T. Childs · Mrs. Thankful Gould · Miss Clara Gould · Miss Mary E. Gould · Mrs. P.S. Wakelee · Mrs. L.M. Green · Mr. A. McHarg & Mrs. Margaret McHarg.

“In many respects those were days of small things, of busy hands, and of home perplexities. Hard work and tired bodies were far more common than cash, and to live as Christ’s men under such circumstances is not only a severe test, but a high privilege.” — Rev. William L. Squier, 39th Anniversary Address, 1906
Nov 1867PastorRev. Samuel Loomis — First Official Pastor

Rev. Samuel Loomis, who had been largely instrumental in founding and fostering the First Presbyterian Church of Vineland, NJ, is employed as the first Minister of the Atco Church. He resides at Atco Ave & 5th Street. His failing health and the languishing state of the settlement bring his work to a standstill early in 1868.

11 Oct 1868SessionFirst Official Session Record of the Atco Church

The Session (Sepional) meets at the call of the Moderator. Present: Rev. E.D. Newberry (Moderator, 4th Presbytery of Philadelphia), and Ruling Elders James E. Alton and Clinton Dewitt Carpenter. Attending as first Building & Grounds Committee: Mr. Peter McHargh · Mr. S. Gibbings · Mr. W.O. Talcott · Mr. Thomas Richards Jr. By letter from Lafayette Avenue Presbyterian Church, Brooklyn, NY: Mr. L.W. Plant and Mrs. Lizzie Plant enrolled.

1868–1869BuildingFirst Church Edifice Built & Dedicated on Raritan Avenue

The Richards Estate donates one acre of ground between Bartram and Clifford Avenues for the church and parsonage. The foundation is laid for a church building 32 feet wide by 50 feet with the frame enclosed. Construction is delayed by the economic depression but is carried through to completion by Rev. E.D. Newberry of the 4th Presbytery of Philadelphia.

On the first Sunday of March 1869, the new church edifice on Raritan Avenue, between Bartram and Clifford Ave., Atco, Camden County, NJ, is dedicated to the worship and Glory of God by Rev. Newberry. Services thereafter held every Sabbath morning at 10½ o’clock; Sunday School at noon.

“Mrs. Deborah (Milhouse Kimber) Richards was a rare woman, and well deserving of the highest tribute of praise; a devoted Christian, a tactful and disinterested worker.”
1869MusicMrs. Adam (Maude) Sloan — First Musician

Mrs. Adam (Maude) Sloan serves as Piano and Choir Director beginning in 1869 — the first recorded musician of the congregation. She will be formally recorded as Organist for Morning or Evening Worship Services in Session minutes on 12 January 1904, confirming her decades-long service.

1868–1873PastorRev. E.D. Newberry — Atco & Berlin

Rev. Newberry oversees the completion of the first church building, dedicates the edifice in March 1869, and shepherds the young congregation through its critical formative years. He serves both Atco and Berlin, with students from Princeton Theological Seminary regularly supplying the pulpit. On 21 September 1868, he takes oversight of the flock and resumes the construction to completion.

5 January 1869: Board of Trustees meets. New members received by profession of faith: Mrs. Julia Giddings, Miss Isabella Hamilton, Mr. Amos Griffins, Mr. Byron Ellsworth. Also by letter from Congregational Church of Springfield, MA: Mrs. Julia Giddings.

1870DenominationOld & New School Presbyterians Reunite

The General Assembly and Presbytery of the USA reunites, ending the Old School/New School split that had existed since 1838. The Waterford Church (Old School) and Atco Church (New School) are now brought together under unified New School policies, rules, and regulations.

1869Local LorePresident Grant Reportedly Visits Atco

Ulysses S. Grant (27 Apr 1822 – 23 July 1885), the 18th President of the United States, is reported to have slept in Atco during one of his visits through the “Garden State” of New Jersey. The specific location is unknown in local records.

1873–1877PastorRev. James G. Shinn — Atco, Berlin & Waterford

4 January 1874: First Session and Congregational meetings held in the “Old Church” on the corner of Bartram & Atlantic Avenue. Rev. Shinn faithfully administers the Lord’s Supper quarterly and guides new members through profession of faith at both Atco and Waterford. In July 1877, Rev. Shinn requests a $1.00-per-week salary increase. The Board of Trustees declines this call, and Rev. Shinn concludes his ministry.

Members received during Shinn’s ministry include: Mr. & Mrs. Adam R. Sloan · Miss Carrie Maria Alton · Mrs. Maria Louisa Franks · Mr. William Sloan · Mr. & Mrs. Conrad & Hannah Kelling & daughter Lizzie · Mr. & Mrs. Christian & Frederika Leman · Mrs. Anna Mary Bishop · Mr. Edward Collings and wife.

1873–1879ContextThe Panic of 1873 — National Economic Hardship

A North American financial panic caused by the Coinage Act of 1873 and a railroad boom creates severe economic hardship. The congregation navigates lean years with remarkable faith — the 10 June 1875 Board of Trustees meeting reports a church balance of just $0.60.

1879CommunityAtco Library & Museum Association Founded; Community Fair Held

The “Atco Library and Museum Association” is organized, with first officers: George W. Hancock (President), Nelson Varnum (VP), Wellington Baker (Secretary), E.C. Scott (Treasurer), and directors including L.W. Plant, A.B. Thatcher, A. Wakeley, B.F. Marshall, and H.G. Tyrell. Its moving spirit is Henry A. Green, whose private collection forms the base.

The “Atco Fair” is held September 5–9, 1879, partly in a tent and partly in the Presbyterian Church (pews removed), featuring fruits, vegetables, needlework by the Atco ladies (including Ladies Guild), and foreign woods, minerals, shells, and fossils. A visiting editor from Vineland notes: “Temperance and industry, with skill and culture, has made this small garden spot in the Jerseys.”

Eleven years later, the organization is reorganized as the “Atco Natural History Society.” A later building on Atlantic Ave between Bartram and Atco Ave, facing the railroad, becomes known as the “Stone Jug.” Based on notes by Dessima Schleinkofer, 1957, written by Arthur D. Pierce.

1888OutreachJuvenile King’s Daughters — Ministry to Hospital Patients

The Juvenile King’s Daughters of Atco, NJ sends a crate of blackberries to patients at Cooper Hospital in Camden, NJ (reported in the Camden County Courier, 28 July 1888). The group takes up an offering at each meeting “for the purpose of building a supply in our treasury that we might be able to send flowers, fruit, card or wrapping to any worthy person in need of cheer.”

MemorialMemorialJames Mac Dougall (1824–1917) — First Ruling Elder of Waterford

Born in Inverness, Scotland on 24 March 1824, James Mac Dougall crossed the sea in 1859, settling in America as Superintendent of a Seed Farm at Waterford, NJ. His family grew to six sons and six daughters. When the Waterford Church was organized on 25 April 1866, he served as Trustee. He was ordained as Ruling Elder on 5 November 1869 and served faithfully for 40 years — visiting Atco every Sabbath when Communion was administered after Elder Carpenter departed in 1876.

He died suddenly of heart failure on Friday night, 2 March 1917, while on his knees in prayer at his home in Waterford, at nearly 93 years of age. His funeral was conducted by Rev. Charles G.E. Chilton. He is interred at Batsto Mill Cemetery. Dated 17 April 1917, signed by Frances J. Collier.

1867–1906RecordsOriginal Session Minutes Book 1867–1928 — Complete Membership

The original First Presbyterian Church of Atco Session Minutes Book (1867–1928) records 136 persons received in the first 39 years: 113 through profession of faith, 13 by letter from Presbyterian churches, 10 from other denominations. Early members documented include: Loomis · Hamilton · Carpenter · Alton · Gould · Wakelee · Green · McHarg · Giddings · Ellsworth · Van · Beebee · Sloan · Holbeck · Schleinkofer (Theodore, Dessima, Susan, Christina, Mary, John, Elizabeth, Lana, Evelyn, Norman, Maretta, Ruth) · Varnum (C. Eddy, Nelson, Joseph, James, Ella, Clarence) · Day · Wynocker · Turrell · Bodine · Gifford · Ottinger · Conrad · Shendock · Regn · Frankle · Brodbeck · Githens · Holton · Hand · Richards · Battelle · Chorpenning · and many more.

1883BuildingOld Church Extensively Remodeled

During the ministry of Rev. J.R. Gibson (Princeton Seminary student), major renovations are carried out at the Old Church on Bartram & Atlantic Ave: re-plastering with two coats, a partition at the west end creating a vestibule, new closet, stairs to the cellar, repaired chimney, repaired seating, matting on aisles, new coal stove, and organ repairs. Total cost: $268.75 ($232.39 from subscriptions; the balance from Sunday collections). Subscription includes $14 from the Ladies Aid Society and $84.99 from the Synodical Missionary, Rev. Allen H. Brown. An original photograph of the interior of the Old Church is later found by Mrs. Donald (Joan) Knoll.

4 Apr 1884Building“Now Having a More Inviting Appearance”

Session records note the church as “now having a more inviting appearance” with 15 members in the congregation but a “large and prosperous” Sabbath School. The membership roll is revised. Miller B. Scott, who had mislaid his New York Presbyterian letter but had regularly attended and desired to serve, is received into membership.

1885–1887CommunitySt. John’s Episcopal Mission Uses the Church for Sunday Evenings

From fall 1885 through summer 1887, the Brethren of St. John’s Protestant Episcopal Mission, under Rev. De Witt C. Loop of Hammonton, hold Sunday evening services in the Presbyterian Church on the corner of Bartram and Atlantic Ave. The Presbyterians and Brethren share the janitor (Mr. Thoraces Orr) cost — $0.50 each, alternate months. At peak: Presbyterians 10 members, Episcopal 8 members, across 16 Sundays. The Mission closes in 1887.

21 May 1887BuildingFire Strikes the Old Church

A major fire breaks out on Sunday while the janitor is lighting the lamps for the evening service. The blazing curtains in the rear of the altar are torn down and thrown out the window before the flames spread. The Camden County Courier reports the incident. Rebuilding follows: nine thousand shingles ordered immediately for a new roof (August 1887), and a Belfry voted to be placed upon the church (September 1887) with other front improvements.

“We will still move forward for God’s work in Atco.” — Annual Church Meeting, Oct 1887
7 Oct 1889FaithSeven Men Save the Church from Permanent Closure

A severe financial crisis nearly causes the church to close permanently. Total cash on hand: $13.25. Seven of God’s honorable men pool their personal resources, donating a combined $165 to ensure the congregation has a place to worship:

Mr. Adam R. Sloan $50 · Mr. Thomas Richards Jr. $25 · Mr. William H. Triol $25 · Mr. George Schleinkofer $20 · Mr. Charles A. Woodland $15 · Mr. W.O. Talcott $10 · Mr. Stephen Van $10

“We will still move forward for God’s work in Atco.”
1890–1891Faith LifeYoung People’s Society of Christian Endeavor (Y.P.S.C.E.) Founded

Under Rev. D.I. Camp as temporary pulpit supply, the Young People’s Society of Christian Endeavor (Y.P.S.C.E.) is organized on 22 February 1891 “for Christ and the Church.” First meeting: 25 March 1891. The pledge: “Trusting in the Lord Jesus Christ for strength, I promise Him that I will strive to do whatever He would like to have me do; that I will make it the rule of my life to pray, and to read the Bible every day…”

Active members include: Theodore Schleinkofer · Theresa Day · Emma Duble · Grace Riley · C.E. Varnum · Lillian Chorpenning · Dessie Hand · Grace Riley. Activities include picnics at Clementon Lake Park, musicals, Union meetings with the Waterford Society, and missions support including contributions to the N.J. Christian Endeavor Union and the Board of Home Missions.

18 Jun 1892TraditionFirst Recorded Church Picnic — Clementon Lake Park

The Y.P.S.C.E. holds its first official picnic at Clementon Lake Park, Clementon, NJ. The wagon departs from the Atco House at 7–7:30 AM. This launches a beloved tradition of Annual Sunday School Picnics that will draw 150 or more attendees in later years, moving between Clementon Lake Park, Centerton Park, and Mays Landing.

20 Aug 1892MembershipSchleinkofer Family Received into Full Communion

Mr. George Schleinkofer (formerly Roman Catholic) and Mrs. Frederica (Freda) Schleinkofer (formerly Lutheran) are received into full communion on confession of faith. The Schleinkofer family will become one of the most influential in the church’s entire history, spanning generations of service.

Dec 1897TraditionSchleinkofer Christmas Candy Tradition Begins

George and Frederica Schleinkofer of Atco begin the cherished tradition of providing one hundred pounds of candy and one box of oranges to the entire church congregation at Christmas — a generous annual gift that becomes one of the most beloved traditions of the congregation.

1898StewardshipNumbered Envelope System & Structured Prayer Meetings

Under Rev. Josiah T. Stevenson, the Board of Trustees unanimously adopts the Numbered Envelope System for church tithing. Weekly Prayer meetings occur alongside a three-month study of “Life of Christ” from International Lessons. A US postage stamp costs $0.01. Presbyterial contribution for Per Capita Tax is $0.40 per member by December 1902.

Oct 1903YouthJunior Society of Christian Endeavor Reorganized Under Mrs. Schleinkofer

The Junior Society of Christian Endeavor is reorganized and presided over by Mrs. Theodore (Dessima) Schleinkofer. Session records describe her devotion as “heroic patience and perseverance” in shaping children’s lives. The Junior CE Society becomes “an invaluable factor in molding the lives of the parish children into lives of larger and fuller usefulness for Christ and the Church.”

10 Aug 1903CommunityChurch Bell Restored — Labor of Love by Varnum & Woodland

Thanks are extended to Mr. Joseph W. Varnum and Mr. Charles A. Woodland for their “labor of love” in repairing the church bell and restoring it to call worshippers to divine service.

1904–1911PastorRev. William L. Squier — 39th Anniversary Historical Address (1906)

8 August 1906: Annual Sunday School Picnic at Clementon Lake Park draws 150 attendees.

18 November 1906: Rev. Squier delivers the Historical Address at the 39th Anniversary of the organization of the First Presbyterian Church of Atco.

18 January 1910: Mr. Thomas Richards Jr. (29 Apr 1828 – 18 Jan 1910) passes from this earth. A letter of appreciation is sent, thanking him and the Richards Estate for nearly 30 years of financial assistance — “doing always the next thing in order that the Church might not die.”

1906TributeProfessor Henry A. Green — Pillar of the Early Church
“A friend and worker that deserves to be remembered as one who contributed largely and ostentatiously to the Church and Sunday School. It was to his wise and indefatigable exertions that the school and its library became such an efficient factor for education and usefulness to the Church and to the community at large.” — Rev. Squier, 39th Anniversary Address

By 1906, Prof. Green lives in Tryon, North Carolina, having moved from Atco in December 1882. As founding Secretary and Treasurer of the Board of Trustees, he shaped the educational mission of the congregation from its very beginning.

1891–1906Sunday SchoolSunday School Ledger Records — Generations of Families

An original Sunday School Ledger records detailed attendance from July 1891 through January 1906. Teachers include: Rev. J. Stevenson · Mrs. Theodore Schleinkofer · Miss M. McHarg · Miss Mary Schleinkofer · Miss Carrie Hand · Miss Emma Duble · Mrs. James Hand · Mrs. Chorpenning. Student families: Schleinkofer · Varnum · Peacock · Rukaber · Duble · Giddings · Koehler · Regn · Brodbeck · Heebner · Offerberger · Shoemaker · O’Neal · Chorpenning · Hurry · Clements · Squier · McCormick · Hand · Stadelmaier · Day · Shendock · Frankle · Lefler and many others.

27 Sep 1916CommunityThe Ladies’ Guild Formally Organized

A new society — The Ladies’ Aid, soon renamed “The Ladies Guild” — is organized to meet on the first and third Tuesday of each month in members’ homes at 2:30 PM. All meetings begin with Scripture reading and the Lord’s Prayer. The Guild becomes an indispensable pillar of church life for generations.

Activities over the decades include: Oyster Suppers · Strawberry Suppers · Tureen (Covered Dish) Suppers · St. Patrick’s Suppers · Coin Calendar drives · Bazaars · regular Building Fund donations. Key officers include Presidents: Mrs. C.B. Githens · Mrs. Charles Holton · Mrs. B.A. Hand · Mrs. John Schleinkofer · Mrs. H. Wyle · Mrs. Theodore Schleinkofer. In March 1964, renamed “United Presbyterian Women’s Association.”

1915–1916BuildingPlans & Lot Secured for a New Church Edifice

12 October 1915: Preparations are presented for a proposed new house of worship. Rev. C.D. Cook is instrumental. Mr. Adam R. Sloan offers the services of architect Mr. Joshua Jefferis of Camden, NJ, who charges only carfare. Theodore Schleinkofer circulates the subscription list.

January 1916: A diagram of the lot on Atco Avenue & Second Street is conveyed by Mr. William A. Murray of Atco, NJ for $2,000. October 1911: On motion of Mrs. Theodore (Dessima) Schleinkofer, the Board of Trustees deposits $100 from the Treasury into a new building fund, followed by $211.40 from the Ladies Guild Christmas Bazaar in Comanche Hall.

Feb 1922CommunityBluebirds Founded — “Spreading Joy in the Name of the Lord”

The “Work & Fun Club” (members of Mrs. John Schleinkofer’s Sunday School Class) and a new organization called “Bluebirds” are both organized in February 1922. The Bluebirds’ first meeting has 16 members sign the constitution. By June 1923, membership grows to 33. Motto: “Happiness to Others.” The Bluebirds first play handbells publicly in the 1980s, performing “Morning Has Broken.” This ministry is still active in 2026.

23 Oct 1921BuildingCornerstone Laid for the New Church Building

The cornerstone of the new church is laid at the Atco Avenue & Second Street location. A list of items placed within the cornerstone is preserved for historical review. On December 5, 1921, the first Church & Congregational Meeting is held in this new building.

9 Jul 1922BuildingNew Church Dedicated & Rev. Muthard Installed

At 10:30 AM, a Service of Dedication of the “New Church” on Atco Avenue & Second Street is held. Rev. R.E. Muthard (recent Princeton Seminary graduate) is installed as pastor. Rev. Robert I. MacBride, Ph.D., Moderator of West Jersey Presbytery, presides. A monumental chapter after decades of planning, fundraising, and faithful sacrifice. On 3 December 1922, the church unites with the Atco Methodist Church for a special Men’s Service — the first recorded ecumenical interaction.

30 Nov 1922Anniversary55th Anniversary — Birthday Gifts & Dedications

The 55th Anniversary is celebrated with birthday gifts totaling approximately $500. Gifts include: a beautiful Oak Communion Table (memorial to the late Joseph R. Varnum, former Elder) · Two pianos presented by the Christian Endeavor Society and the Bluebirds · Additional Communion cups and tray by the Men’s Class · Beautiful chairs purchased by various classes · Two Oak pedestals as a “Memorial” by the congregation.

16 Mar 1923DedicationBaptismal Font Dedicated — In Memory of Miss McHargh

A beautiful baptismal font is dedicated in loving memory of Miss Margaret (Maggie) J. McHargh, whose family had faithfully served the church from its very founding as members of the original Board of Trustees.

2 Mar 1925MissionsMissionary Society Organized

A Missionary Society is organized on 2 March 1925. Original Roll Books are kept from 8 April 1925 through 1936. In 1966, the church supports a Missionary from Thailand and Missionary Mr. Henry Ducher.

9 Jan 1926MembershipDr. Henry C. Schwartz Family Joins the Congregation

Dr. Henry C. Schwartz joins by letter from the First Lutheran Church in Johnstown, PA. Mrs. Mary Schwartz joins by letter from Emmanuel Presbyterian Church of Philadelphia, PA. Dr. Schwartz becomes the only Medical Doctor in Atco for years, renowned for his house calls. During the Depression, he accepts “Pies & Eggs” as payment. Their home and office are on Almira Avenue. Mrs. Schwartz joins the Ladies Guild in 1929 and serves as Guild Treasurer beginning 7 January 1931. Their children: Mrs. Drusilla (Schwartz) Lynch (ca.1927–1983) and Mr. Henry Clay Schwartz Jr. (“Doc”).

1927BuildingA Manse Erected Next to the Church

A manse is built next door to the new church on Second Street. Rev. Joseph Harrison, who serves from 1927–1929, becomes the first pastor to occupy it — a new chapter of stability in pastoral ministry.

17 May 1926CommunityWaterford Township School Graduation Held in the Sanctuary

The Waterford Township School Graduation Exercises are held in the church sanctuary — demonstrating the church’s important role as a central gathering place for the broader community.

31 Mar 1928RecordsOriginal Session Minutes Book 1867–1928 Closes

The original First Presbyterian Church of Atco Session Minutes Book (1867–1928) closes — a treasure covering 61 years of session meetings, member receptions and dismissals, baptisms, communions, and major decisions. A new book continues. The Waterford Presbyterian Church Session Minutes Book runs from 1859 through 1959.

1923–1928CommunityAnnual Picnics at Alcyon Park; Evangelistic Services

1 August 1923: Annual Sunday School Picnic held at Alcyon Park in Pitman, NJ. 7 March 1924: Rev. Thomas Hauston invited to conduct a two-week Evangelistic Service, 5 Oct–24 Oct 1924. Sunday School picnics during the 1920s draw large attendance from Atco, Waterford, and surrounding communities.

Pre-1950TraditionPalm Sunday “Hosanna Parade” Tradition Established

Traditionally every Palm Sunday, a generous church member donates live green Palms. All children parade around the Church Sanctuary while the congregation sings “The King Draws Near” (music and lyrics by Ida Wilson). This beloved tradition continues through the decades.

1929–1936PastorRev. Caleb L. Pancoast — Steady Leadership Through the Depression

Rev. Caleb Pancoast begins 3 April 1929. Despite the Great Depression (1931–1936), church records note: “In spite of economic conditions — our Church was in a good financial condition — we were able to reduce our indebtedness through the generosity and hard work of the members.” Sunday School picnics continue at Centerton Park and Mays Landing. Ladies Guild suppers (Oyster Suppers $0.50/person, Strawberry Suppers, Jigsaw Suppers, Tureen Suppers, Coin Calendar drives) fund steady Building Fund contributions of $50–$100 per meeting.

6 Apr 1930MusicPipe Organ Dedicated — Gift of Theodore Schleinkofer

A Pipe Organ is dedicated at the evening Worship Service, donated by Theodore Schleinkofer. The Session resolution states: “We are profoundly grateful to Mr. Theodore Schleinkofer, a highly esteemed Elder of our Church, for the very generous gift…beautiful in both tonal qualities and appearance.” A copy of the resolution is published in the local newspaper. The old organ is sold in October 1935 for $25. (Note: The Pipe Organ gift is formally acknowledged by Session resolution dated 30 March 1930.)

30 Mar 1930Faith LifeSession Formally Honors Theodore Schleinkofer’s Organ Gift
“We, the Session for the First Presbyterian Church of Atco, New Jersey are profoundly grateful to Mr. Theodore Schleinkofer, a highly esteemed elder of our Church, for the very generous gift which he is making to the Church. That gift, which is a pipe organ, is beautiful in both tonal qualities and appearance. It is our earnest prayer that it will prove to be a most valuable asset in building up the work of the Master’s Kingdom. It is also our prayer that the donor’s life, family and all his interests will be abundantly favored by Providence.” — Mr. John L. Ritter, Clerk of Session, pro tem
28 Mar 1935MusicHandel’s Messiah First Performed by the Senior Choir

G.F. Handel’s “Messiah” (Hallelujah Chorus, page 77) is performed for the first time by the Senior Choir under Mrs. Theodore (Dessima) Schleinkofer. This sacred musical tradition is repeated in 1938 · 1956 · 1969 · 1977 · 1989 — a cherished legacy of sacred music spanning more than half a century.

18 Nov 1930GovernanceSession Passes By-Law on Standards of Reverence in God’s House
“The Session, feeling that it is their duty to uphold Christian standards of reverence in God’s House, have passed a By-Law Statement.”
Nov 1932Anniversary65th Anniversary — Newspaper Coverage & Fellowship Supper

The Breeze & Saturday Evening News reports: “Scene of Anniversary Celebration at Atco Presbyterian Church — Organization Affected 65 Years Ago — Dr. Eubank Preaches Sermon at Impressive Service — Original Structure Erected by Volunteer Labor.” A Good Fellowship Supper is served by the ladies in the Social Center on Tuesday evening.

1934–1946StewardshipAnnual Coin Calendar Contribution Drive

75 Coin Calendar cards are distributed to all members and non-members each year. Yearly ingathering events feature covered dish suppers with guest entertainers (including the Hammonton Presbyterian Church Orchestra directed by Dr. Wood). Typical annual collections: $97–$107 for the Building Fund. First proposed by Mrs. Pancoast in November 1933; cards obtained from 1515 Monroe Street, York, PA.

1937–1940PastorsRev. John C. Castle Jr. & Rev. Charles L. Cureton

Rev. John C. Castle Jr. serves Atco 1937–1940. Rev. Charles L. Cureton serves Waterford 1939–1945 during the years leading toward unification.

21 Dec 1932FamilyThe Congdon Family — Married & Settled in Atco

Mr. John Congdon (21 Nov 1907 – April 1992) of Tacoma, NJ marries Miss Ella (Endres) of Atsion (7 Oct 1910 – 21 Dec 1989), ceremony officiated by Rev. W. Gordon Hannaway. They settle in Atco in 1936. Children: Louetta (Congdon) Phillips (b.30 Sep 1933, married John Phillips 7 Apr 1956) · John Jr. (b.17 May 1935) · George (b.Nov 1937, married Gertrude (Trudi) Passarella, Nov 1968) · Joy Diane (born and passed Nov–Dec 1945) · Virginia (Ginny) Congdon Scharr (b.22 Feb 1947, baptized by Rev. Hannaway, married Elmer Scharr 26 Jun 1965 by Rev. Mont). 10 grandchildren as of 2017. Mr. John Congdon elected Ruling Elder 22 May 1960.

20 Sep 1940PastorRev. W. Gordon Hannaway — 23 Years of Legendary Ministry

Rev. W. Gordon Hannaway begins a 23-year pastorate (through 1 October 1963). Key milestones under his leadership:

November 1942: Church mortgage burned — a celebrated milestone of financial freedom. 100 Man Sundays are legendary in local church history. 1945: Men’s ministry plans the “Hannaway Rooms” to enlarge the church for the growing Sunday School. 1959: Men’s group expands the Manse on Second Street, builds a two-car garage, and paves the parking lot. The Chancel Choir is built up and expanded. Membership grows significantly under his warm, Christ-centered leadership.

1944DenominationBerlin Presbyterian Church Dissolved — Members Welcome at Atco

The First Presbyterian Church of Berlin, NJ (first reported 1868) is dissolved and dropped from the denominational roll in 1944. Its membership roll is invited to worship at Atco Church on Atco Ave. Related archival materials held at the Presbyterian Historical Society, 425 Lombard Street, Philadelphia, PA.

1946–1951MusicMr. Davis Long — New Pipe Organ Installed; Westminster Choir School

A new Pipe Organ is installed. Mr. Davis Long of the Westminster Choir School is hired as Choir Director (1946) and Organist (1947), serving until 1951. He also teaches Visual Arts and Piano. Mrs. Elizabeth Mossop of Atco serves as Organist until 1947.

1950sMusicCarillon Bell System Installed — Easter Sunrise Tradition

A carillon bell speaker system is installed in the church tower, donated by the Brimfield family. Organist Mr. Eugene Ewan plays “Christ the Lord is Risen Today” for the entire community to hear at 6 AM on Easter Sundays — a triumphant tradition heard throughout the Atco neighborhood. Mr. Ewan serves 1951–1954 before being drafted into military service.

1954MusicFirst Allen Organ Installed — Zook & McFerran Begin Dual Service

In 1954, the first “Allen Organ” is introduced. Mr. Joe Zook of Atco serves as Organist from 1954 until his sudden passing in 1981 — 27 years of faithful service. Simultaneously, Mrs. Lawrence (Irene Schleinkofer) McFerran of Atco serves as Organist from 1954 to 1974 (born 1926, died 1976). In fall 1958 for a few months, Mrs. Alexander (Mardi) Duble and Mrs. Harriett DeLanzo serve as sub-Organists.

1950s–1977MusicJr. Choir Directors — Mardi Duble, Then Gail Passarella

Mrs. Alexander (Mardi) Duble of Atco serves as Jr. Choir Director from the 1950s until 1977. From 1977 through the present, Gail (Passarella) Del Monte of Atco (now Berlin, NJ) continues this ministry — a combined span of more than 50 years of faithful service directing the children’s voices of the congregation.

22 May 1960LeadershipNew Ruling Elders: Mr. Snyder & Mr. Congdon Elected

New Ruling Elders elected: Mr. Edward W. Snyder and Mr. John Congdon — both serve until 1963. 13 December 1962: Newly elected Deacon: Mr. Edward (Ed) Snyder and Mr. David (Dave) Brogden. 22 January 1967: New Ruling Elders elected: Mrs. Elizabeth D. Ray and Mr. Edward Snyder. Deacons elected/installed: Mr. David W. Brogden and Mrs. Mary Rapp. Mr. Snyder continues to serve as Ruling Elder through 1981 (Class of 1984).

15 Jan 1962SessionJoint Session & Trustees Meeting — Key 1962 Leadership

Joint Meeting of Session & Trustees at 8 PM, chaired by President Lou Zoppel. Present: Mr. Lou Zoppel · Mr. Fred (Fritz) Holton · Mr. Norman Duble · Mr. Guy Ranck · Mrs. Norman (Franny) Duble · Mr. Harold Ellsworth · Mr. Warren Jensen · Mr. Walter Giddings · Mr. Edward Snyder · Mr. N. Giddings · Mr. John Congdon · Mr. Art Ward · Mr. Harold Ellsworth · Mr. Zan Duble. Topics: how organizations can raise money for the Church, member motivation. Motion to replace the church basement floor; decision to hold four joint meetings per year.

Nov 1963CommunityFirst Ecumenical Community Thanksgiving Service Held

The first recorded Ecumenical Community Thanksgiving Service includes all acting Clergy of the Atco Methodist Church on Atco Ave, Assembly of God Pentecostal Church on Jackson Road, and Atco Assumption Roman Catholic Church (now Christ the Redeemer Roman Catholic Church). Also included: Loving Grace Alliance (until it closed). This tradition continues to the present day.

1966–1967MissionsInternational Mission Support — Thailand & Camden

1966: The congregation supports a Missionary from Thailand and Missionary Mr. Henry Ducher. 1967: Rev. Stanley J. Mont serves as Director of the West Jersey Presbytery “Camden Composite Missionary,” teaching “Life Skills” to small children in Camden, NJ.

1950s–1960sYouthVacation Bible School & Youth Programs Flourish

Vacation Bible School (VBS) is held each summer under pastoral care with Mrs. Mary Rapp as director. Mission Day Trips are taken to “Ranch Hope for Boys,” where youth leaders enjoy Bible study, board games, and cards. These formative programs shape multiple generations of young Christians in Atco and surrounding communities.

1940–1960MembershipWaterford & Atco Membership Rolls — Mid-Century Families

5 Feb 1979SessionSession Meeting 1979 — Congregation & Leadership

Session Meeting, Moderator: Rev. Jack L. Robinson. Present: Mrs. Norma (Holloway) Cook · Mrs. Karen (Duble) Schlachter · Mr. Stan Banes · Mr. Norman Duble · Mr. A.A. (Zan) Duble. Dec 1963–Dec 1968PastorRev. Stanley J. Mont — Atco & Waterford United

Rev. Stanley J. Mont serves as pastor of both Atco and Waterford. During his service: Senior Choir Director/Organist: Mr. Joe Zook; Junior Choir Director: Mrs. Mardi Duble; VBS held each summer with Mrs. Mary Rapp as director. 11 March 1964: The Ladies Guild is renamed “United Presbyterian Women’s Association.” The organization later disbands in the 1980s after decades of faithful service.

1964HistoricAtco & Waterford Churches Officially United as ONE Congregation

Under the direction of the Presbytery of West Jersey, moderated by Rev. Stanley J. Mont, the Atco and Waterford congregations, sessions, and all related affairs are officially united as ONE during a Sunday Morning Worship Service. Nearly a century of shared ministry through circuit preaching — Atco, Waterford, and Berlin serving together since 1867 — is formally consummated into a single united congregation. Respectfully submitted by Rev. Stanley J. Mont (March 2017).

1967OrganizationWaterford Church Building Sold

The original “First Presbyterian Church of Waterford” building is sold. Proceeds deposited into the Atco Church General Fund. The two congregations, now united for three years, fully consolidate at the Atco Avenue & Second Street location.

Oct 1969–Jul 1980PastorRev. Jack L. Robinson — Second Longest-Serving Pastor

Rev. Jack Robinson and his wife Jo lead the church for over a decade — the second longest pastorate in church history. Under his leadership: Praise Dancers ministry is launched · The Mariners, a very active young adult group, is established · The church begins working more with local indigent homes · Contemporary music is introduced using a Gaither Family Songbook · A new hymnal, “Hymns for the Family of God,” is purchased for the congregation.

1977–1992MusicHandbell Ministry Launched — The “12th Bell Choir Fund”

The “12th Bell Choir Fund” is suggested and completed by Mrs. Karen (Duble) Schlachter and Ms. Gail Passarella. Church membership donates each and every bell until the Basic Set is purchased. Through the 1980s and 1990s, individual donations complete the full bell set.

First public handbell performance during Sunday Morning Worship Service: The Bluebirds (1980s, exact date unknown), performing “Morning Has Broken,” directed by Gail Passarella. The Senior Bell Choir later plays at the opening and during special music under Mrs. Cindy Sorge. In 1992, the ministry is renamed “Atco Christ Chimes.”

1981–1984PastorRev. Gary Pemberton, then Rev. Edward Napier (Interim)

Rev. Gary Pemberton serves 10 May 1981–15 August 1983. Rev. Edward Napier serves as Interim Pastor September 1983–September 1984. During 1983, ceiling fans are installed in the sanctuary in loving memory of Mrs. Emma Fuhrer, and the church tower is refurbished.

1922–PresentCommunityBluebirds — A Century of Spreading Joy (Still Active 2026)

Founded February 1922, the Bluebirds have served for over a century. Their mission: “Spreading joy in the name of the Lord.” By sending cards, messages of encouragement, flowers, homemade applesauce or bread, they create a Christian connection with shut-ins and those in need. They meet the last Thursday of each month at 1:30 PM (September through Spring). All women of the church are invited. Still faithfully serving in 2026.

30 Sep 1984PastorRev. Dr. Joseph E. Beggs Ordained — Longest-Serving Pastor (1984–2023)

At 3:00 PM on 30 September 1984, Rev. Dr. Joseph E. Beggs is ordained and installed as pastor of the Atco United Presbyterian Church. His nearly four-decade ministry transforms the congregation:

Spiritual programs: Koinonia (small home group Bible Studies) · Thursday morning Bible Study in Fellowship Hall · Monthly coffee house · Second Sunday morning contemporary service at 8:15 AM and traditional service at 11:00 AM added · September 2022: Combined 9:30 AM service.

Physical improvements: Nancy Fitzpatrick Bus purchased, Food Bus Ministry organized by Sara Troendle (now under Nancy Fallon & Deacons) · Fellowship Hall basement remodeled (asbestos removed, old wooden floor replaced) · New organ purchased for sanctuary · Sanctuary painted and re-carpeted · Land purchased from former Richards Estate behind/next to the Manse (Church Office) · Air-conditioning installed · Accessibility lift added · New accessible entrance and restrooms — all through the Faith Fund overseen by Don Harbert.

Jul 1990Faith Life“Perseverance in Prayer” (PIP) Page Introduced by Mrs. Doris Gordon

The original “Perseverance In Prayer Page” (PIP Page) is introduced by Mrs. Chuck (Doris) Gordon and included in every Sunday morning bulletin. The original format remains in use today — a lasting symbol of the congregation’s commitment to intercessory prayer for one another.

1984–PresentFaith LifeKoinonia Ministry — Small Group Bible Studies Flourish

Under Rev. Dr. Beggs, the Koinonia Ministry (Greek for “Fellowship”) launches and grows stronger over the decades. Small group Bible Studies meet in different homes throughout Atco and surrounding areas. Each group focuses on four areas: fellowship, prayer, Bible study, and mission/ministry. The Thursday morning Bible Study in Fellowship Hall — “Never too old to learn, even today” — also continues.

1990s–PresentOutreachFood Bus Ministry — Free Groceries & Prayer to Those in Need

The Nancy Fitzpatrick Bus is purchased and the Food Bus Ministry organized by Sara Troendle, now under direction of Nancy Fallon and the Deacons. Monthly ministry to Greater Calvary Baptist Church in West Atco, NJ: free bags of groceries, a smile, and a time of prayer. Still active in 2026 — next scheduled: Greater Calvary Baptist Church, West Atco, NJ.

30 Apr 2017Anniversary150th Anniversary Celebrated

The 150th Anniversary of the United Presbyterian Church of Atco is celebrated with a 10 AM Worship Service followed by a Church Supper in Fellowship Hall — a joyful milestone marking a century and a half of faithful witness in the Atco community.

2020–2021YouthKids4Christ & Tots4Christ Programs Launched

February 2020: Kids4Christ launches for ages 4–14 on the third Friday of each month — a two-hour faith-based program modeled after VBS: eat, play, sing, laugh, and share in the joy of Jesus Christ. 2021: Tots4Christ begins for parents and children ages 0–4 on the second Friday of each month (September–June, paused summers): Bible Study, singing praises, snack, and playtime in Fellowship Hall.

Jul 2022CommunityFinal Vacation Bible School — Held at Christ The Redeemer

VBS is held in July 2022 at Christ The Redeemer Roman Catholic Church with “Community Christians Churches of Atco.” Theme: “Mega Sports Camp.” Under Pastor Beggs (37 years of service) and Gail (Passarella) Del Monte (after 50 years), this proves to be the final season of the church’s VBS program.

Sep 2022TodayTwo Services Combined — Single 9:30 AM Worship

In September 2022, the 8:15 AM Contemporary and 11:00 AM Traditional Sunday morning services are combined into a single blended 9:30 AM Contemporary & Traditional Worship Service — welcoming all members together in one unified celebration of faith.

23 Jul 2023PastorRev. Dr. Joseph E. Beggs Retires After 39 Years
